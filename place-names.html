World Building & Naming Tips

Establish Naming Conventions

Create consistent patterns for different cultures or regions in your world.

Mountain dwellers might use hard consonants, while coastal people use softer, flowing sounds.

Consider Historical Context

Think about the history of your locations and how that might influence their names.

Places might be named after founders, historical events, or geographical features.

Use Descriptive Elements

Incorporate geographical features, colors, or notable characteristics into place names.

Examples: "Crimson Peaks," "Whispering Woods," "Stonebridge."

Reflect Cultural Identity

Different cultures in your world should have distinct naming styles.

This adds depth and makes your world feel more authentic and diverse.

Research Real Languages

Draw inspiration from real-world languages and their naming conventions.

Old English, Latin, Norse, and Celtic languages provide excellent foundations.

Match Tone & Genre

Ensure your place names fit the overall tone of your story or game.

Dark fantasy might use ominous names, while high fantasy uses more epic, majestic names.
