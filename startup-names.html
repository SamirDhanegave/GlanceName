Proven Naming Strategies

Descriptive Names

Clearly explain what you do

Names that directly describe your product, service, or industry. These names are straightforward and help customers immediately understand your business.

General Motors
The Weather Channel
Booking.com
Food Network

Best for: B2B companies, service businesses, and industries where clarity is crucial.

Evocative Names

Create emotional connections

Names that evoke feelings, imagery, or concepts related to your brand. These names are memorable and can build strong emotional connections.

Amazon
Nike
Uber
Slack

Best for: Consumer brands, tech companies, and businesses wanting to stand out.

Create unique brand assets

Completely new words created specifically for your brand. These names are highly distinctive and easily trademarkable.

Verizon

Best for: Tech startups, innovative products, and global brands.

Acronym Names

Best for: Professional services, technology companies, and organizations.

Best for: Consulting firms, creative agencies, and businesses with well-known founders.

Essential Naming Tips

Check Availability Early

Before falling in love with a name, verify that the domain name and social media handles are available.

Use tools like domain registrars and namecheckr to check availability across platforms.

Consider Global Appeal

Ensure your name works internationally and doesn't have negative connotations in other languages.

Test your name with people from different cultural backgrounds if you plan to expand globally.

Think About Trademarking

Choose a name that's distinctive enough to be trademarked and protected legally.

Avoid generic terms that are difficult to trademark and protect from competitors.

Keep It Simple

Short, simple names are easier to remember, spell, and share with others.

Aim for 1-3 syllables and avoid complex spellings or confusing pronunciations.

Allow for Growth

Choose a name that won't limit your business as it grows or expands into new products/services.

Avoid overly specific names that might become irrelevant as your business evolves.

Test with Your Audience

Get feedback from your target customers to ensure the name resonates with them.

Pay attention to their emotional reactions and associations with the name.

Startup Name Checklist

Essential Criteria for Your Startup Name

  • Memorable: Easy to recall and share with others
  • Meaningful: Relevant to your business and target audience
  • Simple to Spell: Easy to type and find online
  • Available: Domain and social media handles are obtainable
  • Scalable: Works as your business grows and expands
  • Trademarkable: Distinctive enough for legal protection
  • Positive Connotations: Creates good associations
  • Pronounceable: Easy to say and share verbally
  • Brandable: Works well with logos and visual identity
  • Future-proof: Won't become dated or irrelevant

Common Naming Mistakes to Avoid

Being Too Literal

Overly descriptive names can limit your business and make it harder to stand out from competitors.

Example: "San Francisco Web Design Company" is too specific and lacks brand appeal.

Ignoring Domain Availability

Failing to check domain availability can lead to expensive acquisitions or settling for poor alternatives.

Solution: Always check domain availability before finalizing your name.

Copying Competitors

Choosing names similar to competitors creates confusion and limits your brand's uniqueness.

Example: Creating "Facepage" when Facebook exists creates immediate comparison.

Overcomplicating Spelling

Creative spellings might seem unique but can make your name difficult to find and share.

Example: "Kreative" instead of "Creative" causes spelling confusion.

Neglecting Cultural Sensitivity

Names that work in one language or culture might have negative meanings in others.

Solution: Research your name across different languages and cultures.

Rushing the Process

Choosing a name too quickly can lead to regrets and expensive rebranding down the road.

Solution: Take time to research, test, and validate your name choice.
