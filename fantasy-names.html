Tips for Creating Memorable Fantasy Names

Consider Phonetics

Think about how the name sounds when spoken aloud. Heroic names often have strong consonants, while mystical names might have softer, flowing sounds.

Avoid names that are difficult to pronounce or remember.

Establish Cultural Roots

Create naming conventions that reflect your world's cultures and histories.

Different regions or races should have distinct naming patterns that make sense within your world-building.

Match Name to Personality

The name should reflect the character's personality, background, and role in the story.

A noble knight might have a strong, traditional name, while a mysterious sorcerer might have an exotic, unusual name.

Research Real Languages

Draw inspiration from real-world languages and mythology to create authentic-sounding names.

Old English, Norse, Celtic, and Latin can provide excellent foundations for fantasy names.

Create Naming Patterns

Establish consistent patterns for different cultures or families in your world.

This adds depth and makes your world feel more real and internally consistent.

Test for Memorability

Choose names that are distinctive enough to be remembered but not so unusual that they're distracting.

Say the name out loud and see how it feels when you imagine calling the character.
